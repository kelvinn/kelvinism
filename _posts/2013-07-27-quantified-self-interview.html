--- layout: post title: Quantified Self Interview date: '2013-07-27T16:44:00.002+10:00' author: Kelvin Nicholson tags: - articles - quantified self modified_time: '2013-07-27T16:44:50.480+10:00' thumbnail: https://blogger.googleusercontent.com/img/b/R29vZ2xl/AVvXsEiC9Rqg935yk28KMHOmsVtoQ6CL6UtLubOVrYphTiS1P09_9rGdtv3LSUmjE2TYOUWuhKNCITwG5ktH1R45mUXUYlUgaIVwPXHvdu29-uZF9lsD3y8Sb5dt6i9t7-a5CDg3esOtfkEINwLJ/s72-c/HeartRateInterview.png blogger_id: tag:blogger.com,1999:blog-3439832858234004835.post-6690221317438861874 blogger_orig_url: https://www.kelvinism.com/2013/07/quantified-self-interview.html --- YS and I were recently interviewed about self-tracking and Quantified Self by one of the major news channels in Australia. I will reflect on the experience after the show has aired, but it was an overall great experience. We have a new respect for filming what may ultimately be just a two minute segment. Depending on how the editing is done it will either provoke the hosts to contemplate the value of a data-centric macroscopic view of the world, or give them lots of fodder.

That said, as you would expect, I had to track my heart rate during the interview - see below. My interpretation is that my heart rate jumped at the start of every questions, and went down as I answered the question. It also dropped when the interview finished. I wish I had a more expensive heart rate monitor (e.g. Zephyr BioHarness or Scanadu) that tracked skin temperature and breathing. My hands felt cold by the end.
